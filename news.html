26 November 2014
Econ Platform

After having successfully monitored the estuary environment for two years, eCon platform is temporarily out of the estuary. We will add an asset monitoring device to the eCon platform and put it back into the estuary. Therefore live data will be temporarily unavailable. The assset monitoring device is currently operating from the lab and its live data is avaialbe under Asset Monitor tab.

18 September 2013
Data Analysis

Dr Jessica Craig has taken up the challenge of analysing and interpreting all the data produced by the eCon platform up to September. This amounts to over 200,000 datasets of calibrated instrumentation readings and high resolution camera images, almost one terabyte of data in total. Dr Craig will be based at the University of Aberdeen Oceanlab facility for this six month study.

11 July 2013
Spring Tide Maintenance

The oxygen sensor readings have been trending lower since the 3rd of July due to sand build-up around the platform. The readings are now back to normal after a spring tide allowed time for relocating the platform and cleaning the sensors.

The Spring surge of biological growth in the Ythan Estuary has abated and the eCon camera is now providing clearer photos. There is a local colony of over a thousand common seals and some are regularly visiting the platform just before high tide, mostly in the hours of darkness. One of the seals is pictured here taking a close look at the camera.

03 May 2013
Challenges of an Estuarine Environment

For this pilot study the eCon observatory is installed in an estuarine environment. Although the ease of access is useful for a pilot study, the site presents a number of challenges that would not normally be present at a subsea site such as the deep ocean or arctic:

During spring with inputs of nutrients and sunlight the Estuary bursts into life with high levels of biological production. As such, during this period the eCon observatory and all it's sensors are covered with biological growth. Although generally the sensors are tolerant to this growth the camera window does become obscured at times. In collaboration with Oceanlab (University of Aberdeen) a programme of camera window cleaning helps to reduce this effect. However, adding to this issue during these periods of high production the water is full of particles further reducing camera visibility (observe the turbidity sensor readings). In practice near subsea infrastructure in the deeper ocean where there is little or no natural light this effect of biological growth obscuring the camera window is greatly reduced.

At our pilot site in the Ythan Estuary during the summer, autumn and winter when biological production slows the biological growth on the camera window and water visibility will improve.

12 April 2013
Data Interpretation

At low tide the camera and some of the sensors are above water resulting in measurements being taken in air rather than in water and thus the results during these exposed periods must be understood in this context.

28 March 2013
eCon Observatory Installation

A very low spring tide and good weather allowed for a safe and successful eCon installation in the Ythan estuary. The chosen pilot site is adjacent to the University of Aberdeen 'OceanLab' facility which will provide power and network connections for the duration of the project. We extend our thanks to all OceanLab staff for their assistance during test and deployment. The eCon observatory will now go through a period of data verification before public release.
